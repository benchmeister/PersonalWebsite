Who I Am

Hey — I'm Wei Feng, a Year 2 Information Security student at the Singapore Institute of Technology (SIT), currently interning at Ensign Infosecurity on the Security Testing & Red Team track. I spend most of my time breaking things professionally, competitively, and out of curiosity.

I'm part of N0H4TS, a CTF team based in Singapore, and previously served as Vice President of CYBERH4TS — SIT's cybersecurity club.

What I Do

Red Team & Penetration Testing

Day-to-day at Ensign I work on security testing engagements — Active Directory attacks, network penetration, evasion tradecraft, and writing up findings that actually help clients fix things. I hold the OSCP, OSEP, and OSWP certifications and am always pushing further.

Threat Intelligence

I'm drawn to understanding the adversary — not just the TTPs but the why. I write AI-assisted threat intel deep dives on APT groups, tracking how nation-state actors evolve their tooling and stay under the radar. Check out my post on UNC3886 for an example.

CTF Competitions

Competing in CTFs keeps the fundamentals sharp. I focus on binary exploitation, web, and active directory / Windows challenges. Playing with N0H4TS, we target regional and international competitions.
